OUR STORY

To our founder Dani, being an older sister to three brothers means more than making them play dress up until they were old enough to realize they could say no. When Justin, the oldest of her brothers, was born with a wide array of disabilities, the two of them began a lifelong journey of mutual love and inspiration. Neither would have guessed it would result in the creation of Peak, but when Justin grew into a young man, he struggled to get hired in a meaningful job despite his hard work and the hard work of those who support him. Seeing this firsthand, Dani knew it was time for change. The realization that the unique contributions of countless individuals are being passed up on inspired her to form Peak Innovation Inc. to provide resources for and promote the participation of people of all ability levels in design.

OUR WORK

While many important efforts seek to give immediate aid and relief to people of different abilities in need, our approach seeks to provide long-term solutions to poverty and unemployment by integrating community, educational and infrastructural resources, and an adaptable skillset to empower people of different abilities to move forward in a way that brings their unique talents to the world. Based in Thailand, we have been working at schools for the disabled for eight months to co-create a makerspace to educate and connect students to the world of design. We believe that true accessibility does not mean taking what exists and retrofitting it to the differently abled as an afterthought, but designing with inclusivity as the inspiration for our work. We are constantly developing our ideas and designs based off of the needs of our students, who themselves are the driving force behind the innovation taking place. Our space is intended to be an incubator of creativity, confidence, and progress toward a better world for all, connecting students to the tools and resources they need to build a life for themselves and those around them.

OUR VISION

We believe that no one knows what people with different abilities need better than the people who live with them every day. Currently most opportunity in the design and implementation of accessible technology is limited to able bodied people.  Peak seeks to change that. By equipping people of different abilities with a foundational confidence in design and manufacturing processes, the beneficiaries of Peak Innovations can make exactly what they need to in order to empower them to do whatever it is they want to do. Whether or not their long-term goals are to become full-time designers and engineers, by empowering differently abled people to create technology that helps them live their everyday lives or gets them closer to their ultimate career goals, substantial improvement can be made in the standard of living for all. Accessibility is more than a feature, and it’s time to integrate a new generation of makers whose voices are deeply needed in design.
